Syllabus: Fundamentals of Artificial Intelligence
 (AI)
1. Introduction to Artificial Intelligence
Definition, concept, scope, importance, and evolution of AI.
2. Definitions and Perspectives of AI
John McCarthy’s definition, Russell and Norvig’s definition, AI as intelligent agents.
3. Goals and Objectives of AI
Learning, reasoning, problem solving, knowledge representation, perception, decision-making, and
NLP.
4. Types of Artificial Intelligence
Narrow AI, General AI, and Super AI.
5. Core Components of AI Systems
Data, algorithms, and models.
6. Fundamental Areas of Artificial Intelligence
Machine Learning, Knowledge Representation, Reasoning and Inference, Search, NLP, Computer
Vision, Robotics.
7. Machine Learning in AI
Supervised, unsupervised, and reinforcement learning.
8. Search and Problem Solving
State space representation and search strategies.
9. Knowledge Representation and Reasoning
Logical reasoning and inference mechanisms.
10. Artificial Intelligence Agents
Concept of agents, agent–environment interaction, types of agents.
11. AI vs Machine Learning vs Deep Learning
Relationship and differences.
12. Applications of Artificial Intelligence
Healthcare, finance, education, transportation, manufacturing, customer support.
13. Advantages of Artificial Intelligence
Automation, accuracy, efficiency, and availability.
14. Limitations of Artificial Intelligence
Cost, data dependency, lack of creativity, ethical concerns.
15. Ethical and Responsible AI
Bias, fairness, transparency, privacy, and accountability.
16. Future Scope of Artificial Intelligence
Explainable AI, Edge AI, human–AI collaboration, responsible AI.

17. Summary
Role, potential, and challenges of AI.
